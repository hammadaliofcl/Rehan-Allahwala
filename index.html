Rehan Allahwala

"Man who wants to end poverty in the World"

Rehan Allahwala was born in Pakistan, a country of 230 million people. Coming from an entrepreneurial family, his father taught him to be independent. He sold candies in his neighborhood at 8, stationary to his schoolmates at 10, and started his first company selling Commodore 64 games at 13. He later made modems and voice digitizing devices at 16 and had done 51 different ventures by the time he was 36, when he retired and started teaching entrepreneurship via the internet.

With the money he makes from his telecom company, he started a foundation to help people find and fulfill their dreams through entrepreneurship. He also established a school to teach basic literacy to a billion people through mobile phones, as part of his ultimate goal of ending poverty worldwide.
